Catch-all usually refers to a mailbox on a domain that will “catch all” of the emails addressed to the domain that do not exist in the mail server. Configuring a catch-all address can help avoid losing emails due to misspelling.

For example: if catchall@example.com is configured to be the catch-all email address on example.com, then any email sent to an undefined @example.com email address will be forwarded to catchall@example.com instead of being rejected as an Undeliverable message or Unknown User error.

 

insert into pop3_catchall (email_user,username,sc_domain) values ('_email_','_account_user_','_Domain_');

Example:

Domain : owensullivan.com

Username : owensull

Email User: victoria

 

insert into pop3_catchall (email_user,username,sc_domain) values ('victoria','owensull','owensullivan.com');
