Customer Communication 

Before you reply to any ticket, be sure you check if there is any grammar, spelling and formatting error. 
Formatting mistakes can be identified by reading replies before sending to customers.

Customer Focus

We should analyse each ticket and check if they comply with our quality control requirements.  If we have completely resolved the issue, then it should be "Adequate resolution". ( we expect each resolution should be AR)


Tickets that do not comply with our quality control requirements are considered:
1) If customer asked multiple questions and we didn't address them all, then it will be "All questions not replied to".
2) If the issue is temporarily resolved and re-occuring, then the resolution is "Incomplete Resolution".
3) If we ask for unnecessary details in ticket (like asking for login when customer already gave it in ticket), that reply will be rated as "Need not have asked for more info".
4) If the customer query was wrongly replied or solution is incorrect, rate the reply as "Resolution is Incorrect".
5) If we haven't provided proof or the proof is not valid/adequate, rating will be "Proof not provided/Inadequate proof".

 In this Customer Communication includes following aspects.

1) Reply should be free of grammatical/spelling/Formatting mistakes.
2) Reply should be simple without much technical jargon.


Customer Focus should cover following:

1) Avoid Incorrect Resolution
2) Provide Complete resolution
3) Do Adequate followup
4) Adequate proof of the resolution.

 

Internal Procedures

1) Tech should follow procedures properly.

2) Add summary and customer details in ticket notes
3) Abuse and follow up procedure 
4)  Correct followup replies in each scenario.
